Hero & Navigation Visual Treatment (Critical)

The hero section must be full-width and should define the entire top of the page.
Do not use borders to contain or separate the hero. The hero should rely on width, spacing, and background—not borders—to establish structure.

The hero and top navigation must feel like a single, unified visual block, not two separate sections.

The navigation bar must sit inside the hero background, not on a white header or detached container.

Do not visually separate the navbar from the hero using borders, lines, or background color changes.

The transition from navigation to hero content must be seamless, as if they belong to one continuous section.

Hero Container (Full-Width Priority)

The hero background must span 100% of the viewport width.

Remove all borders entirely.

Use large border-radius only on the outer hero container edges (soft, rounded corners) if needed for polish—not as a boxed or bordered card.

The hero should feel expansive and immersive, not constrained.

Navigation Style

Navigation should be transparent or semi-transparent, allowing the hero background/image to subtly show through.

Navigation text should be light-colored (white or off-white) for strong contrast.

Logo aligned to the left.

Menu items aligned center or right.

Primary CTA button (“Book a Cleaner”) styled as a pill-shaped button with a contrasting background.

Hero Layout & Composition

Hero background should use a full-bleed image carousel across the entire hero width.

Apply a soft dark gradient overlay for text readability.

Hero content should be left-aligned:

Small eyebrow text (short tagline)

Large, bold H1 headline

Supporting subtext below

The headline should feel confident, premium, and authoritative.

CTA Buttons

Place two CTA buttons directly beneath the hero text.

Primary CTA:

Solid background

Fully rounded

Highly prominent

Secondary CTA:

Outline or subtle background

Fully rounded

Buttons should align horizontally on desktop and stack vertically on mobile.

Visual Depth & Polish

Use subtle shadows or elevation to give the hero depth—never borders.

Optional: very light glassmorphism or blur effects (keep extremely subtle).

Avoid sharp edges, harsh lines, or boxed layouts.
